## Keep Your Air Fryer Spotless: How To Clean Grease Easily

My air fryer is a kitchen workhorse. I use it almost daily for crispy fries, juicy chicken, and quick snacks. But with all that delicious food comes a common problem: grease.

Grease builds up inside the basket, drawer, and even the heating element. This not only looks messy, but it can also affect your food's taste and your appliance's performance. A greasy air fryer can smoke, smell bad, and even become less efficient over time.

Learning how to clean grease from your air fryer is simple. It helps your air fryer cook better and last longer. In this article, I will show you effective ways to tackle grease. We will cover routine cleaning, deep cleaning, and natural solutions. Get ready to make your air fryer shine!

### Takeaway
*   Clean your air fryer regularly to prevent grease buildup.
*   Use dish soap for daily cleaning and stronger solutions for stubborn grease.
*   Natural cleaners like baking soda and vinegar work well for deep cleaning.
*   Always unplug the air fryer before cleaning for safety.
*   Allow all parts to dry completely before reassembly.

To clean grease from your air fryer, first unplug the appliance and let it cool. Then, remove the basket and drawer, washing them with hot, soapy water. For stubborn grease, use a paste of baking soda and water or a vinegar solution. Wipe the interior with a damp cloth, ensuring all parts are dry before use.

## Why Your Air Fryer Gets Greasy and Why Cleaning Matters

Air fryers cook food by circulating hot air. This process makes food crispy and delicious. However, as food cooks, especially fatty items, grease can splatter. This grease sticks to the basket, drawer, and heating element. Over time, these grease spots become sticky and hard to remove. I have seen this happen often with chicken wings or bacon.
