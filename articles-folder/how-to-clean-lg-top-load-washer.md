## Deep Clean Your LG Top Load Washer: A Complete Guide

Does your laundry come out smelling less than fresh, even after a wash? Perhaps you have noticed a musty odor or a slimy film inside your machine. These are common signs that your LG top load washer needs a deep clean. Cleaning your washing machine is not just about aesthetics; it is crucial for its performance and the cleanliness of your clothes.

A dirty washer can transfer grime, mildew, and unpleasant smells to your garments. This makes all your laundry efforts feel pointless. I have personally experienced the frustration of a smelly washer, and I learned that regular cleaning is the key. In this comprehensive guide, I will show you exactly how to clean your LG top load washer, step by step. We will cover everything from using the built-in Tub Clean cycle to tackling stubborn residues in the dispenser and agitator. You will learn how to make your washer sparkle and keep your clothes smelling wonderfully clean.

### Takeaway

To effectively clean your LG top load washer and maintain its performance:

*   Regularly use the dedicated "Tub Clean" cycle.
*   Clean the detergent dispenser and fabric softener compartments often.
*   Manually wipe down the drum and agitator to remove residue.
*   Keep the washer lid open after cycles to promote air circulation.
*   Use appropriate cleaning agents like bleach, vinegar, or specialized washer cleaners.

### Main Query Answer

To clean your LG top load washer, run the "Tub Clean" cycle with a cleaning agent like bleach or a washing machine cleaner. Manually scrub the drum, agitator, and detergent dispenser with a solution of vinegar and water or baking soda to remove residue, mold, and odors, ensuring all parts are dry afterward.

### Why Your LG Top Load Washer Needs Regular Cleaning

Have you ever wondered why your clothes sometimes do not smell as fresh as they should, even after a wash? The answer might be your washing machine itself. Over time, detergents, fabric softeners, minerals from hard water, and lint can accumulate inside your LG top load washer. This buildup creates a breeding ground for mold, mildew, and bacteria. These unwelcome guests cause unpleasant odors that transfer directly to your laundry.

A dirty washer also performs less efficiently. Residue can clog dispenser nozzles and interfere with the washing process. This means your machine works harder to get clothes clean, potentially using more energy. Regular cleaning ensures your LG washer continues to operate at its best. It extends the life of your appliance, preventing costly repairs down the line. I always think of it as a preventative measure. Just like you clean your dishes, you need to clean the machine that cleans your clothes. Neglecting this maintenance can lead to a musty smell that permeates your entire laundry room.

### Gathering Your Cleaning Essentials

Before you begin the cleaning process, it is wise to gather all your supplies. Having everything ready saves time and makes the task much smoother. You likely have most of these items already in your home. I always make sure I have these on hand for my cleaning days. The primary cleaning agents we will use are common household staples.

You will need:

*   **Bleach:** Regular household chlorine bleach is excellent for sanitizing and killing mold. Always use it in a well-ventilated area.
*   **White Vinegar:** Distilled white vinegar is a fantastic natural cleaner. It dissolves mineral deposits, breaks down soap scum, and neutralizes odors. You can read more about its versatile cleaning power here: [How To Clean With Vinegar And Baking Soda](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-with-vinegar-and-baking-soda).
*   **Baking Soda:** This is another powerful odor absorber and gentle abrasive. It helps scrub away grime and freshens your machine.
*   **Microfiber Cloths or Sponges:** You will need these for wiping down surfaces and scrubbing. Have a few on hand so you can dedicate one for dirtier areas.
*   **Small Brush (e.g., an old toothbrush):** This is essential for getting into tight crevices, especially in the detergent dispenser.
*   **Spray Bottle:** Handy for mixing and applying vinegar solutions.
*   **Optional: Washing Machine Cleaner:** Products like Affresh or OxiClean Washing Machine Cleaner are specifically designed for this task. They can be very effective, especially for deep-seated grime. These can be used in place of or in conjunction with bleach.

Make sure your washer is empty of clothes before you start. This allows the cleaning agents to work directly on the machine's surfaces.

### The LG Tub Clean Cycle: Your First Step

Your LG top load washer comes equipped with a fantastic feature designed specifically for cleaning: the Tub Clean cycle. This cycle is your primary tool for maintaining a fresh and odor-free washer. It is like an automatic spa treatment for your appliance. I always run this cycle regularly to keep my machine in top condition.

The Tub Clean cycle uses higher water levels and vigorous agitation to thoroughly rinse the inside of the wash tub. It helps flush away detergent residue, fabric softener buildup, and any mold or mildew that might be clinging to the drum walls. LG recommends running this cycle at least once a month, or more frequently if you do a lot of laundry or notice odors.

Here is how to use it:

1.  **Ensure the Washer is Empty:** Do not put any laundry in the machine when running the Tub Clean cycle.
2.  **Add Cleaning Agent:**
    *   **Using Liquid Bleach:** Add 1 cup of liquid chlorine bleach directly into the dispenser or main wash compartment. Do not mix it with other cleaners in the dispenser.
    *   **Using Powdered Washing Machine Cleaner:** If you are using a specialized washing machine cleaner, follow its instructions. Usually, you place the tablet or powder directly into the wash drum.
    *   **Using Vinegar:** Some prefer using 2 cups of white vinegar for a natural approach. Add it directly to the drum or through the dispenser. While vinegar is great for general cleaning and dissolving mineral deposits, bleach is more effective for sanitation and killing mold.
3.  **Select the Tub Clean Cycle:** On your LG top load washer's control panel, look for the "Tub Clean" or "Clean Washer" button or setting. Select it.
4.  **Start the Cycle:** Press the "Start/Pause" button to begin the cycle. The machine will fill, agitate, soak, and drain automatically. This cycle can take a significant amount of time, sometimes over an hour, depending on your model. Let it complete fully.

After the cycle finishes, wipe down the inside of the drum with a clean cloth to remove any remaining residue. This step is important for a truly clean finish. Running this cycle consistently will prevent many common washer problems.

### Deep Cleaning the Washer Drum and Agitator

While the Tub Clean cycle does a lot of heavy lifting, some areas of your LG top load washer require manual attention. The drum and the agitator can accumulate stubborn grime and residue that the water jets alone cannot dislodge. This manual cleaning step ensures a thorough clean, reaching spots that might otherwise be missed. I find that this step truly makes a difference in preventing musty smells.

Here is how to deep clean these components:

1.  **Prepare a Cleaning Solution:** Mix a solution of equal parts white vinegar and warm water in a spray bottle. For tougher spots or mold, you can create a paste with baking soda and a little water. You can explore how these two ingredients work together in various cleaning tasks by checking out [How To Clean With Vinegar And Baking Soda](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-with-vinegar-and-baking-soda).
2.  **Spray and Wipe the Drum Interior:** Lightly spray the inside of the washer drum with your vinegar solution. Pay close attention to any visible streaks, discoloration, or slimy residue. Use a clean microfiber cloth to wipe down all surfaces. If there are stubborn spots, apply your baking soda paste, let it sit for 5-10 minutes, then scrub gently.
3.  **Clean the Agitator (if applicable):** Most LG top load washers have an agitator column in the center. Residue can build up around its base and in any crevices. Spray the agitator thoroughly with your vinegar solution. Use a small brush or an old toothbrush to scrub around the base and any grooves. Wipe it clean with a cloth.
    *   *Note:* Some LG top-loaders are impeller models, which have a low-profile plate at the bottom instead of a tall agitator. Clean this impeller plate the same way, paying attention to the edges and any openings.
4.  **Check Under the Rim:** Carefully lift the lid and inspect the underside of the drum's top rim. This area often collects lint, soap scum, and mold due to moisture. Spray with vinegar solution and wipe diligently with a cloth. Use your small brush for tight spots.
5.  **Rinse (Optional but Recommended):** After scrubbing, you can run a short rinse cycle or a quick wash cycle with no detergent to thoroughly flush away any remaining cleaning solution and loosened grime.
6.  **Dry Thoroughly:** Once cleaning is complete, always leave the washer lid open for several hours, or even overnight. This allows air to circulate and thoroughly dry the interior, preventing new mold and mildew growth. This is a critical step I never skip.

This manual cleaning, especially after a Tub Clean cycle, provides a comprehensive attack on all dirt and odors within your washer.

### Tackling the Dispenser and Detergent Compartments

The detergent and fabric softener dispensers are often overlooked parts of your LG top load washer, yet they are prime locations for grime and mold to accumulate. Residue from concentrated detergents and fabric softeners can become sticky and harbor bacteria, leading to unpleasant smells and inefficient dispensing. I always target these areas, as they can cause visible mildew. Cleaning these compartments is fairly straightforward and makes a big difference.

Here is how to clean them:

1.  **Remove Removable Parts:** Many LG top load washer dispensers have removable trays or inserts. Gently pull these out. They usually slide or clip into place. Check your washer's manual if you are unsure how to remove them. Removing them allows for thorough cleaning.
2.  **Soak and Scrub:**
    *   **For Mildew/Soap Scum:** Place the removed dispenser parts in a sink filled with warm water. Add a generous splash of white vinegar or a few tablespoons of baking soda to the water. Let them soak for about 15-30 minutes to loosen any dried-on residue.
    *   **Scrub:** Use a small brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any soap scum, fabric softener buildup, or visible mold. Pay attention to the corners, crevices, and dispenser nozzles where residue can hide. I often find black mold spots here, which are easy to remove with a good scrub. You can learn more about how to clean mold from various surfaces, including parts of a washing machine, by visiting [How To Clean Mold From Washing Machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-mold-from-washing-machine).
3.  **Clean the Dispenser Housing:** While the removable parts are soaking, inspect the area inside the washer where the dispenser sits. This housing can also collect residue. Use a damp microfiber cloth and your small brush to wipe and scrub inside this area. Spray a little vinegar solution if needed.
4.  **Rinse and Dry:** Thoroughly rinse all the cleaned dispenser parts under running water. Make sure all soap and cleaner residue is gone. Dry them completely with a clean cloth before reinserting them into the washer. If any moisture remains, it encourages mold growth.
5.  **Reassemble:** Carefully slide or clip the dry dispenser parts back into their original positions. Ensure they are securely in place.

Regularly cleaning the dispenser prevents clogs and ensures your detergent and fabric softener are dispensed properly during cycles. I recommend wiping down these areas quickly after every few washes to prevent heavy buildup.

### Cleaning the Washer's Exterior and Control Panel

While the interior of your LG top load washer takes the brunt of dirt and odors, do not neglect the exterior. A clean exterior contributes to the overall freshness of your laundry area and protects your appliance. Dust, spills, and general household grime can accumulate on the surface and control panel. Keeping the outside clean also prevents dirt from potentially finding its way into the washer drum. I always finish my washer cleaning routine by tidying up the exterior; it makes the whole machine look new again.

Here is how to clean the exterior surfaces and control panel:

1.  **Prepare a Gentle Cleaning Solution:** For the exterior, a mild all-purpose cleaner or a solution of warm water and a few drops of dish soap works perfectly. You can also use a diluted white vinegar solution (1 part vinegar to 1 part water).
2.  **Wipe Down the Top and Sides:** Dampen a clean microfiber cloth with your chosen cleaning solution. Wipe down the entire top surface of the washer, including the lid, and the side panels. Pay attention to any spills or sticky spots. For stubborn stains, you might need to apply a bit more pressure or let the solution sit for a minute.
3.  **Clean the Control Panel:** The control panel is sensitive, so be careful not to oversaturate it with liquid. Dampen a clean cloth (do not spray directly onto the panel) with your cleaning solution. Gently wipe the buttons, knobs, and display screen. If your panel has crevices around buttons, use a cotton swab or a small brush to get into those areas. Never use abrasive cleaners or scrubbers, as they can scratch the display or damage the finishes.
4.  **Clean the Lid Underside:** Do not forget the underside of the washer lid. This area can collect dust and condensation. Wipe it down thoroughly with a damp cloth.
5.  **Polish (Optional):** If your LG washer has a stainless steel finish, you can use a stainless steel cleaner and a clean, dry microfiber cloth to polish it. Always wipe in the direction of the grain to avoid streaks.
6.  **Dry Thoroughly:** After cleaning, use a dry, clean microfiber cloth to wipe down all surfaces. This removes any lingering moisture and prevents streaks. Leaving surfaces dry prevents dust from sticking quickly and deters water spots.

A clean exterior not only looks good but also reflects good appliance care. It is a quick step that completes your comprehensive washer cleaning.

### Maintaining Your LG Top Load Washer for Long-Term Freshness

Cleaning your LG top load washer once is great, but maintaining its cleanliness is key to avoiding future odors and issues. Consistent habits ensure your machine stays fresh and performs optimally. I have adopted these practices over time, and they have significantly reduced the frequency of deep cleaning needed. These simple steps can extend the life of your appliance and keep your clothes smelling consistently clean.

Here are essential maintenance tips:

1.  **Leave the Lid Open After Each Wash:** This is perhaps the most important tip. After every laundry load, leave the washer lid open for at least a few hours, or even overnight. This allows air to circulate inside the drum, helping it dry completely. Moisture trapped in a closed washer creates the perfect environment for mold and mildew to thrive, leading to that common musty smell.
2.  **Use High-Efficiency (HE) Detergent:** Always use detergents specifically formulated for HE washers. These detergents are low-sudsing and designed to work efficiently with less water, preventing excessive suds and residue buildup. Too much regular detergent can leave a gummy film in your machine.
3.  **Use the Right Amount of Detergent:** More detergent does not mean cleaner clothes. Using too much detergent leads to excess suds and residue, which can cling to the drum and internal components. Follow the manufacturer's recommendations for detergent dosage based on load size and soil level. I always measure my detergent carefully.
4.  **Wipe Down After Each Use:** A quick wipe around the top rim of the drum and the detergent dispenser after you finish doing laundry for the day can prevent buildup. This takes only a minute but makes a big difference.
5.  **Remove Clothes Promptly:** Do not leave wet clothes sitting in the washer after a cycle. This creates a damp, warm environment that encourages mold and mildew growth in both your clothes and the machine. Transfer clothes to the dryer or hang them immediately.
6.  **Clean the Dispenser Regularly:** Even if you do not do a full washer clean, periodically remove and rinse the detergent and fabric softener dispenser trays. You can typically do this quickly with warm water.
7.  **Run a Tub Clean Cycle Monthly:** As mentioned earlier, make a habit of running the Tub Clean cycle at least once a month. Set a reminder on your phone or calendar. This proactive step prevents significant buildup before it becomes a problem.

By incorporating these simple maintenance habits into your routine, you will keep your LG top load washer smelling fresh and performing efficiently for years to come.

### Troubleshooting Common Washer Cleaning Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter a few persistent issues with your LG top load washer. A lingering odor or stubborn mold spots can be frustrating. Do not worry; there are often straightforward solutions to these problems. I have faced these challenges myself and learned some effective ways to tackle them. Understanding these common issues can help you get your washer back to its best.

Here are some common washer cleaning issues and their solutions:

1.  **Lingering Musty Odor:**
    *   **Cause:** Deep-seated mold/mildew, especially in areas not fully reached by the Tub Clean cycle, or consistently leaving the lid closed.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Bleach Shock Treatment:** Run another Tub Clean cycle, but this time use 2 cups of liquid chlorine bleach. Let it complete fully. Bleach is highly effective at killing odor-causing bacteria and mold.
        *   **Vinegar Soak (No Laundry):** Fill the washer with the hottest water setting. Add 4 cups of white vinegar. Let it agitate for a few minutes, then pause the cycle and let the vinegar water sit in the drum for 30 minutes to an hour. Resume the cycle and let it drain. Follow with another rinse cycle.
        *   **Check and Clean Drain Pump Filter:** While most LG top-loaders do not have readily accessible user-cleanable lint filters, some models might have a drain pump filter located near the bottom of the machine, often behind a small access panel. This filter can trap lint and debris, causing odors if clogged. Refer to your LG manual for its location and cleaning instructions, as it might require a service professional or specific tools to access safely.
2.  **Visible Mold or Mildew Spots:**
    *   **Cause:** High humidity, trapped moisture, infrequent cleaning. Often found on the top rim, around the agitator base, or in the dispenser.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Baking Soda Paste:** Mix baking soda with a small amount of water to form a thick paste. Apply this paste directly to the mold spots. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. The baking soda helps to absorb odors and provides a gentle abrasive.
        *   **Scrub with Vinegar:** After the paste has sat, spray white vinegar directly onto the paste. It will react with the baking soda, creating a fizzing action that helps lift the grime. Scrub vigorously with an old toothbrush or a stiff brush.
        *   **Bleach Spray:** For very stubborn mold, create a solution of 1 part bleach to 10 parts water in a spray bottle. Spray directly onto the moldy areas, let sit for 5-10 minutes (do not let it dry), then scrub and wipe clean. **Always wear gloves and ensure good ventilation when using bleach.**
3.  **Detergent or Fabric Softener Residue:**
    *   **Cause:** Using too much detergent, using non-HE detergent, or not cleaning the dispenser regularly.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Clean Dispenser Thoroughly:** Remove all dispenser components and soak them in hot, soapy water or a vinegar solution. Use a small brush to scrub all crevices.
        *   **Run Hot Water Cycle:** Run an empty hot water wash cycle (without detergent) to help rinse out any internal residue.
        *   **Adjust Detergent Usage:** Re-evaluate your detergent dosage. Less is often more, especially with concentrated HE detergents.
4.  **Small Black Specks on Clothes:**
    *   **Cause:** These are typically mold or mildew flakes breaking off from inside the washer.
    *   **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive deep clean as outlined in this guide, focusing on the Tub Clean cycle with bleach and manual scrubbing of all surfaces. Repeat the Tub Clean cycle if necessary.

By addressing these specific issues, you can resolve most cleaning challenges and restore your LG top load washer to optimal performance.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: How often should I clean my LG top load washer?**
A1: I recommend cleaning your LG top load washer at least once a month. If you do laundry very frequently, have hard water, or notice any lingering odors, you should clean it more often, perhaps every 2-3 weeks. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and keeps your machine fresh.

**Q2: Can I use both bleach and vinegar to clean my washer?**
A2: No, you should never mix bleach and vinegar directly. This combination creates a toxic chlorine gas that is dangerous to inhale. You can use them separately during different cleaning cycles or at different times, ensuring one is thoroughly rinsed out before introducing the other.

**Q3: What if my LG top load washer still smells after cleaning?**
A3: If a smell persists, it might be due to a deep-seated mold issue or a clogged drain pump filter (check your manual for accessibility). Try running another Tub Clean cycle with bleach, and ensure you are leaving the lid open consistently after washes to allow for air circulation and drying. You might also need to manually clean the dispenser thoroughly.

**Q4: How do I prevent mold and mildew in my top load washer?**
A4: The best way to prevent mold and mildew is to always leave the washer lid open for several hours after each wash cycle. This allows the interior to dry completely. Additionally, use HE detergent sparingly, remove laundry promptly, and clean the detergent dispenser regularly.

**Q5: Do I need to use special washing machine cleaners?**
A5: While specialized washing machine cleaners like Affresh are effective and convenient, they are not strictly necessary. Common household products like liquid chlorine bleach, white vinegar, and baking soda are highly effective and often more economical. The choice depends on your preference and the severity of the buildup.

**Q6: Does my LG top load washer have a filter to clean?**
A6: Most LG top load washers do not have a readily accessible lint filter for users to clean, unlike some older models or front-loaders. If your model has a drain pump filter, it is usually located at the bottom front or back of the washer. Refer to your LG owner's manual for specific instructions on your model's drain pump filter, as accessing it may require technical knowledge.
